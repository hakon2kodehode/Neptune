Neptune

What do we really know about it?

Neptune is widely considered one of the most mysterious and least understood planets in our solar system.

This is largely because it is the only planet that has not been visited by an orbiter.

Its extreme distance—30 times farther from the Sun than Earth makes it difficult to study, leaving many of its internal features and atmospheric behaviors unknown.

Join us as you deep-space dive into a collection of interesting facts and information we do know about arguably the coolest planet in our solar system

Travel to Neptune

What if we wanted to travel to Neptune?

Neptune is about 4.3-4.8 Billion kilometres from the earth. That's pretty far. Even with our fastest modern space-craft it would take around 12 years to arrive there from earth.

This is how long NASA's Voyager 2 spacecraft took to reach Neptune from earth. It was launched in 1977 and arrived Neptune in 1989, travelling at a speed of 19 kilometres a second.

Perhaps in the future with advances to theoretical technologies like Cryogenic freezing, we could more easily preserve and sustain humans for the long journey.

In addition to logistical challenges, cosmic radiation is also a problem. Exposing astronauts to potential lethal radiation.

Cooking on neptune

What would happen if you managed to land on the surface of Neptune, and tried to cook a delicious breakfast?

Technically you cannot “land” on Neptune, since it has no solid surface.

You would instead descend into its thick atmosphere of hydrogen, helium and methane. Which would probably smell like farts.

Long before you would even think to season your bacon and eggs, you & your cast iron frying pan would flash-freeze in an instant due to the planets extremely cold exterior temperature of -210c and shatter due to the immense pressure. All the while you are being bombarded by razor-sharp diamond rain at super high wind-speeds of up to 2,000km/h.

The deeper you descent into the planets interior, the hotter it would become. If there was anything left of you, it would now melt and vaporize as you approach the planets inner core which can reach temperatures of up to 7,000c which is roughly as hot as the surface of the sun.

Finally you would be broken down at a molecular level, fusing and becoming part of the planet itself.

In short, you're cooked.
